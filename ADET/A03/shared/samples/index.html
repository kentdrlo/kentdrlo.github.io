Close-Up (Zoom In)
Focuses tightly on a subject’s face or object. Great for showing emotion or detail. It helps the audience see what the character is feeling. This shot is often used during serious or emotional moments to make the scene feel more personal. It also draws attention to something important. Viewers feel closer to the character or item being shown.
Medium Shot (Half Body)
Shows the subject from the waist up. It’s commonly used in conversations or everyday scenes. This shot balances the character and their surroundings. It helps show both facial expressions and body language. It makes the scene feel natural and easy to follow. It’s one of the most used shots in films and shows.
Wide Shot (Full Scene)
Shows the entire subject along with a large part of the background. It helps set the scene and gives context to where the action is happening. This shot is great for showing movement, space, or group interactions. It makes the character look smaller in the environment. Often used at the start of a scene to introduce the setting.
Low Angle Shot (Bottom Shot)
The camera looks up at the subject from below. This makes the person or object look powerful, strong, or important. It’s often used to show confidence, danger, or authority. It can also make the viewer feel smaller or more nervous. This shot adds drama and makes the moment feel intense.
