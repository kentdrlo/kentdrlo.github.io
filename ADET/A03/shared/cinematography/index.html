"Bringing Stories to Life Through Cinematography"
Cinematography is the art of visual storytelling. Itâ€™s how a film looks, feels, and flows. From camera angles to color tones, everything you see on screen is part of the story. Every shot is carefully planned to show emotion, explain meaning, and make the story feel alive.
Key Elements of Cinematography
Lighting - sets the mood, time of day, and emotional tone.
Camera Movement - smooth or shaky, fast or slow, each motion has purpose.
Color & Tone- warm, cool, vivid, or muted palettes add depth and symbolism.
Framing & Perspective - tells the audience where to look and what to feel.
